# E-Commerce Analytics Dashboard: Case Study

## The Story Behind the Data

Every e-commerce business faces a critical problem: their data is scattered. Sales data lives in Shopify, traffic data comes from Google Analytics, paid advertising performance sits in Google Ads and Meta and customer engagement data resides in email platforms. Teams spend four to five hours every week manually stitching this data together. By the time insights are compiled, the information is already outdated.

This project demonstrates a unified solution that transforms fragmented data into a single source of truth.

## The Problem

### Before: Fragmented Reality

Consider an e-commerce marketing team's typical Monday morning:

- The CEO asks: "How did we perform this week compared to our target?"
- The marketing manager needs to know: "Which channel is actually driving profitable customers?"
- The merchandising team wants to understand: "Which products are underperforming and why?"

Today, the answers take four to five hours to compile. By Wednesday, the data is outdated. Strategic decisions get delayed.

### Root Cause Analysis

Five disconnected data systems created five distinct pain points:

1. **GA4**: Event-level user behaviour and conversions, but no cost data
2. **Google Ads & Meta Ads**: Campaign performance, but no product-level insights
3. **Shopify**: Transaction and product data, but no traffic source attribution
4. **HubSpot**: Customer engagement metrics, but siloed from product performance
5. **Manual reporting**: 4.5+ hours weekly reconciling data across platforms

The result was no single view of what actually drives profitability.

## The Solution: Integrated Analytics Architecture

### Five-System Integration

The dashboard ingests data from all critical touchpoints:

- **GA4 integration**: User behaviour, conversion funnels and audience segments
- **Shopify connection**: Product performance, order values and merchandise insights
- **Google Ads & Meta**: Channel-level ROAS and customer acquisition cost
- **HubSpot data**: Retention metrics and email-attributed revenue
- **Looker Studio**: Unified visualisation layer with automated reporting

Data flows into a hierarchical dashboard structure that serves three distinct stakeholder groups simultaneously. This structure is organised across the four-tier KPI framework described below.

### The 4-Tier KPI Framework

Rather than overwhelming users with 100 metrics, the dashboard organises KPIs into a strategic hierarchy:

#### Tier 1: Commercial Health (Executive Level)

Answers: "Is the business on track?"

- Total Revenue vs Target
- Conversion Rate
- Average Order Value
- Blended Return on Ad Spend

#### Tier 2: Acquisition Efficiency (Marketing Level)

Answers: "Where do profitable customers come from?"

- Sessions by marketing channel
- Customer Acquisition Cost by source
- New vs returning user ratio

#### Tier 3: Product Performance (Merchandising Level)

Answers: "Which products drive profitability?"

- Revenue by category and product
- Checkout abandonment by device
- Creative performance metrics

#### Tier 4: Customer Retention (Growth Level)

Answers: "How do we build recurring revenue?"

- Repeat purchase rates (30, 60 and 90-day cohorts)
- Customer lifetime value trends
- Email-attributed revenue percentage

## Business Impact

### Quantified Results

Implementing this analytics architecture across five or more e-commerce clients produced measurable outcomes:

**Time Efficiency:**

- Reduced weekly reporting time from 4.5 hours to 30 minutes (90% time saving)
- Moved from manual to automated weekly reporting

**Data Immediacy:**

- Reduced data lag from five to seven days to near-real-time visibility
- Strategic decisions are now based on current information

**Revenue Impact:**

- Typical conversion rate improvement: 15–22% range (8–12 weeks post-implementation)
- Typical average order value improvement: 10–16% range
- Improvement mechanism: Faster identification of underperforming channels and products enables rapid budget reallocation and merchandising optimisation
- Context: Results reflect optimisation-driven gains in the current economic environment (2024–2026), not traffic-dependent growth
- Variation: Larger merchants (100K+ monthly orders) typically see 10–15% uplift; smaller merchants (10K–50K) often achieve 15–22% uplift due to a greater data visibility gap pre-implementation

**Visibility:**

- 12 actionable KPIs across five organised dashboard pages
- Three distinct stakeholder views (Executive, Marketing and Merchandising)

## Strategic Insights Uncovered

### Discovery 1: Channel Profitability Varies Dramatically

Initial analysis revealed that while organic search drove 40% of traffic, paid social generated 35% of profitable orders despite costing three times more per click. Without integrated data, teams were optimising for the wrong metric: traffic volume rather than profitable revenue.

### Discovery 2: Product Category Cannibalism

The dashboard revealed that a bestselling product category was cannibilising sales from a higher-margin alternative. This pattern would have remained invisible in siloed Shopify data alone.

### Discovery 3: Device-Specific Checkout Friction

Cross-referencing GA4 user journey data with Shopify conversion data identified mobile checkout abandonment at twice the desktop rate. A subsequent UX improvement lifted mobile conversion performance meaningfully across the affected accounts.

## Why This Matters for Businesses

This project demonstrates the relationship between data accessibility and business performance. When teams can answer critical business questions in minutes rather than hours, decisions are made faster. When those decisions are based on current data rather than week-old reports, outcomes improve.

The architecture is replicable across e-commerce verticals. Fashion, health supplements, consumer electronics and subscription services have all benefited from this framework, as the KPI hierarchy adapts to the specific commercial priorities of each vertical.

## Key Takeaway

Modern e-commerce success is not just about collecting data. It is about making data accessible to the right people at the right time in a format they can act on. This project demonstrates that principle in quantifiable business results.
